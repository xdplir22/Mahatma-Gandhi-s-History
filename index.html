Mahatma Ghandi

Social activist | 1869-1948

Mahatma Gandhi led India's independence movement from British rule and also founded non-violent civil obidence while protesting racial segregation in South Africal. His birthday is now Indian national hoiday, as well as the international Day of Peace

Marraige

Gandhi married his wife, Kasturba, when he was 13, and together they had five children. His family stayed in India while Gandhi went to London in 1888 to study law and to South Africa in 1893 to practice it.

Peace March

Gandhi led the Salt March in protest against the Britain’s Salt Acts, which not only prohibited Indians from collecting or selling salt—a dietary staple—but imposed a heavy tax that hit the country’s poorest particularly hard

Assasinated

Ghandi was killed following a prayer vigil in New Delhi on January 30, 1948. His killer was Nathuram Vinayak Godse, a Hindu fanatic who believed Gandhi had been too conciliatory to the Indian subcontinent’s large Muslim minority.
